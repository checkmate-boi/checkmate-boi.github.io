Chapter 1: The Beginning

I first touched a chessboard at the age of 5. Obviously I wasn’t old enough at the time to comprehend any of its rules, but my older brother knew how to play, and I often watched him face off with my dad. Dad wasn’t especially good, but he always won. My brother would always challenge him for a rematch, probably hoping that one day he might actually win. In the end that day never came, and by the time he entered middle school, chess was only a vague memory in his head, and his chessboard became an antique in his closet.

        I quietly sneaked into my brother's room, and quickly started looking through his things. He would be home in an hour maybe, I had to maximize this time. His desk drawers? No. His cabinet? No. His schoolbag? No. Maybe hidden in his clothing drawers? No.

        Darn it. No Gameboy anywhere. He must've taken it with him today. And I was looking forward to playing Pokemon Silver too.

        sigh~ I lay down on his bed and stared at his ceiling, bored out of my mind.

        Is there nothing to do at all? There’s nothing to watch on the telly. Maybe I can read some comic books from the library? Hm..

        My eyes floated towards the mirror that was in the room, one of those tall mirrors that you could get at IKEA. I hopped off of the bed, and as was almost instinctively for a 6 year old, I began posing in front of it.

        "Fireball!"

        "Hadouken!!"

        Then something caught my eye in the mirror’s reflection. Huh? What's that? I saw something in the closet I'd left open. I turned back to it to check it out. It was my brother's old chess board, untouched for a while. It had piled on a thin layer of dust. There was a small wooden box next to it also covered in dust, likely containing the pieces. I opened it to check, and sure enough, they were all lined up in there, as if they were just waiting for someone to find and use them.
