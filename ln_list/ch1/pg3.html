He retreated to Bishop H5. Of course he would. No need to further press that. Wait but if I actually did try that and it worked, that’d be so awesome. Well here goes...

        Bishop C4.

        With his Bishop now out of harm’s way, he developed a Knight: C6.

        I captured his E5 Pawn with my Knight

        Then almost like a reflex, he immediately took my open Queen with his Bishop. Just as I’d hoped. He looked at me and smiled with an eyebrow raised, as if to taunt me for giving him a free Queen. I simply smiled back.

        I moved my Bishop, capturing his pawn on F7.

        “Check.”

        He tilted his head with a hmm.

        “What?” I asked in a sarcastic tone.

        “Nothing, nothing.” His brow furrowed a little, as if he was in denial that he only had one available option.

        King E7.

        Knight D5.

        “And that’s Checkmate, right?” I asked in a fake monotone voice, trying to not scream in excitement. With my brother having never won against dad, it kind of felt like I had just defeated a final boss in a game or something.

        Dad just sat there looking at the board, deep in thought.

        “That was fun. Rematch?”

        “Y-yeah, sure. Let me refill my tea first.”

        Dad had an expression I hadn’t seen before. It felt like a mixture of confusion and interest.

        “Okay I’ll set up the board again.” I said while resetting the pieces. “This time I’ll play black.”

        I continued to play chess over the following years with my parents, and occasionally my friends. The matches were always pretty short, and I remained the undefeated champion. I never thought that I was especially good or anything, it was simply a hobby of mine. Then in 7th Year, when I entered middle school, I discovered that our school had a chess club.
